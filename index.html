Here is an example QNAmarkup "decision tree" method to elicit "patent classification codes" for each disclosure.

Q(1): Does the innovation you are disclosing apply to "Lighting"? A: Yes. Q(1.1): Does it relate to a Light Source? A: Yes. Q(1.1.1): Great. Your US Classification Code for that aspect of your innovation is D26/1. Is it related to an Electric Light Bulb (34)? A: Yes. Q(1.1.1.1): Great. Your new US Classification Code for that aspect of your innovation is D26/2. Is it Fluorescent? A: Yes Q(1.1.1.1.1): Great. Your new US Classification Code for that aspect of your innovation is D26/3. A: No. Q(1.1.1.1.2): Is it Simulative? A: Yes Q(1.1.1.1.2.1): Great. Your new US Classification Code for that aspect of your innovation is D26/4. Is it simulative of a candle or Flame? A: Yes Q(1.1.1.1.2.1.1): Great. Your new US Classification Code for that aspect of your innovation is D26/5. A: No Q(1.1.1.1.2.1.2): Your US Classification code for this aspect of your innovation is still D26/4. A: No Q(1.1.1.2): Your US Classification code is still D26/1 for this aspect of your innovation. Does it relate to a candle? A: Yes
Q(1.1.1.2.1): Great your new US Classification Code is D26/6. Is it Simulative? A: Yes Q(1.1.1.2.1.1): Great your new US Classification Code is D26/7. A: No Q(1.1.1.2.1.2): Your US Classification code is still D26/6 A: No Q(1.1.1.2.2): Your US Classification code is Still D26/1. Is it a torch? A: Yes Q(1.1.1.2.2.1): Great. Your new US Classification Code is D26/8 A: No Q(1.1.1.2.2.2): Your US Classification code is still D26/1 A: No. Q(1.1.2): Does it relate to a Candle Holder? A: Yes. Q(1.1.2.1): Great your US Classification code is D26/9. A: No Q(1.1.2.2): Okay then. GOTO:2 A: No Q(1.2): Okay then. GOTO:2 Q(2): Head over to FirstToDisclose.org for other options.
